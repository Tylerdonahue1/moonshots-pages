Project Requirements

What to Create

Build a profitable business whose operations are written in plain English and executed by AI agents (each a "Project"). The Project must fall within at least one of the following Categories:

Education & Human Potential
Workforce upskilling, alternative credentialing, personalized learning.
Entrepreneurship & Job Creation
Solo founder enablement, AI-assisted company creation, job matching.
Small Business Services
AI-native operations, cashflow, customer acquisition, compliance navigation.
Money & Financial Access
Credit access, remittances, micro-lending, financial literacy.
Professional Services Access
Legal tools, immigration navigation, mental health support.

Functionality

The Project must be capable of being successfully installed and running consistently on the platform for which it is intended, and must function as depicted in the video and/or expressed in the text description. The Project must also include at least 5 of 12 separate operation playbooks such as:

Sales Onboarding Support Pricing Delivery HR Ops/Logistics Finance Marketing Analytics Compliance Partnerships

Platforms

A Project must be fully hosted on Google Cloud Platform. The Project must run on the platform for which it is intended and which is specified in the Submission Requirements.

New Projects Only

Projects must be newly created by the Entrant after the start of the Hackathon Submission Period. If pre-existing templates, frameworks, boilerplates, or code were used, Entrants must explain how their Project utilized that pre-existing work.

Third Party Integrations

If a Project integrates any third-party SDK, APIs and/or data, the Entrant must be authorized to use them in accordance with any terms and conditions or licensing requirements of the tool.

Submission Requirements

Submissions to the Hackathon must meet the following requirements:

  • Include a Project built with the required developer tools that meets the above Project Requirements.
  • Selection of a Category.
  • Provide a URL to your code repository for judging and testing. The repository must contain all necessary source code, a /playbooks folder, agent configuration, and a Manifest file. The Manifest must list each playbook, which operational category it covers, and one sentence describing what breaks if the playbook is deleted. The repository must be either public (with relevant licensing) or private and shared with testing@devpost.com and email@hackerfund.com.
  • Include a text description that explains how you use the playbooks day to day, what humans do versus what agents do, and the story of building the business this way. This should also explain the relevance to the chosen Category.
  • Include a demonstration video of your Project. The video portion of the Submission:
    • should be less than three (3) minutes — judges are not required to watch beyond three minutes;
    • must demonstrate a live playbook change and the resulting behavior change in the production application;
    • should include footage that shows the Project functioning on the device for which it was built;
    • must be uploaded to and made publicly visible on YouTube, Vimeo, or Youku, with a link provided on the submission form; and
    • must not include third-party trademarks or copyrighted music/material unless the Entrant has permission to use such material.
  • Provide profit evidence that includes:
    • Total Revenue. Total revenue earned from arms-length third-party customers during the Hackathon period, in USD.
    • Revenue by Month. Revenue broken out by calendar month: May, June, July, and August 2026.
    • Total Costs. Total costs incurred during the Hackathon period, excluding marketing and customer acquisition spend (reported separately). Include a one-sentence description of what these costs cover (e.g., hosting, AI API usage, contractor fees).
    • Marketing and Customer Acquisition Spend. Total spend on marketing and customer acquisition during the Hackathon period. Must be disclosed even if zero.
  • Provide Customer evidence that includes:
    • Evidence of real customers — number of individual customers and a high-level breakdown of who your customers are. Provide any testimonials or feedback. Please ensure customers are aware their information is being shared.
    • Related-Party Revenue. Any revenue earned during the Hackathon period from team members, family, related entities, or pre-existing customer relationships. Reported separately from Total Revenue so judges can assess whether the underlying business serves arms-length third-party customers.
  • Provide evidence of your product running. This includes agent execution logs, API usage records, and screenshots of dashboards — anything that strengthens the case that playbooks are running in production continuously.
  • If an organization, must also include their corporate ID.

During the review process you may be requested to provide more evidence related to the above, including customer contact information (name, email, phone), live demonstration requests, and additional financial documentation.

§ 06

Judges & Criteria

Sponsor and Administrator reserve the sole right to determine the eligibility and judging methodologies for all submissions. This process may utilize expert panels, peer review, automated AI-driven analysis, or any combination thereof to ensure efficient, fair, and objective evaluation. Eligible submissions will be evaluated by a panel of judges selected by the Sponsor (the "Judges"). Judges may be employees of the Sponsor or third parties, may or may not be listed individually on the Hackathon Website, and may change before or during the Judging Period. Judging may take place in one or more rounds with one or more panels of Judges, at the discretion of the Sponsor.

▢ Stage One

Pass / Fail Viability

Determines whether the ideas meet a baseline level of viability — i.e., the Project reasonably fits the theme and reasonably applies the required APIs/SDKs featured in the Hackathon.

▢ Stage Two

Equally-Weighted Criteria

All Submissions that pass Stage One are evaluated on the three equally-weighted criteria below, at the sole and absolute discretion of the Judges.

The Three Judging Criteria

01 Profitability

Teams must launch a real business during the Hackathon, acquire real customers, and generate real income. Judges assess both actual profit achieved during the 90-day window and the sustainability of the underlying business model.

02 Extent of AI-Native Operations

Teams must run their business through AI agents executing plain English operations playbooks. Judges assess whether playbooks are live in production, execute key business decisions, and how broadly they govern the operation.

03 Category Impact

Teams built something that meaningfully moves the needle in their chosen category — either by redefining how something works at a fundamental level, or by reaching a scale where widespread adoption is credible.

The scores from the Judges will determine the potential winners of the applicable prizes. The Entrant(s) eligible for a Prize whose Submissions earn the highest overall scores based on the applicable Judging Criteria will become potential winners of that Prize.

Administrator and Sponsor reserve the right to verify any submission and disqualify entries that don't meet requirements. Requests sent via email must be responded to within 2 business days. This may include:

  • A live call to demonstrate your Project and answer questions about your build and business.
  • Additional financial documentation such as revenue records, expense statements, or proof of customer relationships.

Tie Breaking

For each Prize listed below, if two or more Submissions are tied, the tied Submission with the highest score in the first applicable criterion listed above will be considered the higher-scoring Submission. In the event any ties remain, this process will be repeated, as needed, by comparing the tied Submissions' scores on the next applicable criterion. If two or more Submissions remain tied on all applicable criteria, the panel of Judges will vote on the tied Submissions.
