01 — The Night

This is not another summit.

No keynotes you'll forget by the parking lot. No panels about what might happen someday. The Moonshot Gathering is a live competition with real stakes, decided in real time, in front of you.

Ten builders and creators walk on stage. They pitch the future they're trying to make real. The judges weigh in. You weigh in. And before the night is over, the money is awarded and the winners are named — live.

You won't be watching the future get discussed. You'll be helping choose which one gets built.

02 — Future Vision XPRIZE
$0M
One story. Made real.

The winner's film gets made.

Five filmmakers take the stage, each with a three-minute vision of an optimistic, technology-forward future. A panel of directors, actors, and creators weighs in — and so does the room.

The winner doesn't just take home $2.5M. Their film gets made into an actual feature.

Think about what that means. The audience that night helps decide which vision of tomorrow gets pulled out of someone's imagination and put on screen — where it can inspire the people who go build it. Star Trek did exactly that. So did the stories that gave us the cell phone, the tablet, the private spaceflight company.

We're not just rewarding a film. We're incentivizing hope — and betting that the right story, seen by enough people, becomes the blueprint for what comes next.

03 — Gemini XPRIZE Hackathon
$0M
Decided live · $500K to first place

Built now. Scaled next.

Five builders take the stage with solutions to real problems — the kind that could change thousands of lives. A panel of founders and investors weighs in, and the audience helps decide.

One million dollars in prizes, awarded in the room. First place walks away with $500K in cash and the momentum to go further.

These aren't pitches for someday. They're things people built, working now, competing live for the capital to scale.

04 — The Thesis

Here's what the night is really about.

Optimism isn't the belief that everything will be fine. That's naive, and it's lazy. Real optimism is the belief that problems are solvable — paired with the willingness to actually go solve them.

That combination has built every good thing we have. Every cure, every breakthrough, every leap forward started with someone who refused to accept that the problem was permanent.

September 25 is a celebration of those people. The builders and the storytellers. The ones who don't wait for the future — they make it.

Come help us decide which futures are worth building.
