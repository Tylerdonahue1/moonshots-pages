04 · Voice & Tone
Voice
Peer-to-peer. Direct. Ambitious without corporate posture. Punchy and minimal over verbose. Stories, not announcements.
Do
"The crowd picks the future. Your brand powers the moment."
Short, active, punchy. Mission-forward. Makes the sponsor feel central.
Don't
"We are pleased to offer a comprehensive sponsorship solution for your brand's strategic goals."
Corporate filler. Passive voice. Generic. No specificity.
Do
"Fiction as blueprint — stories like Star Trek directly inspired real-world technology. This competition bets the same is true today."
Specific narrative hook. Connects history to now. Earned conviction.
Don't
"Through innovative storytelling and cutting-edge technology, we are shaping tomorrow's landscape."
Vague buzzwords. No concrete claim. Could belong to any brand.
Do
"Content can start in the dark. It just can't stay there."
Acknowledges complexity. Shows editorial maturity. Memorable.
Don't
"Only positive, uplifting content about a bright and beautiful future."
Naive. Oversimplified. Doesn't match the actual competition ethos.
05 · Usage Rules
Rules
🎨

Don't swap teal for gold

Teal (#1BBFE0) is the brand accent. Gold was used in early materials — it's off-brand. All new assets use teal.

🔠

Headlines always uppercase

Display type (Barlow 800–900) is always uppercase. Body and labels use normal casing. Never title-case a Barlow headline.

📋

No bullets in partner-facing copy

Peter Diamandis reads bullets as AI-generated. Any content he'll read or present must be flowing prose.

🤝

XPRIZE brand approval workflow

Co-branded materials require 5-day advance approval. Peter's organic social does NOT. Keep this distinction clean.

🏆

Prize framing

Grand prize is $2.5M equity investment + $100K cash = $2.6M+. Not a "cash payout." Frame as production funding.

📰

Press channels

Tech publications → McKenna. Hollywood trades → Range/Amia. Never cross-wire these two channels.
